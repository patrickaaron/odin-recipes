Aji de gallina

Aji de gallina is a classic Peruvian dish, with shredded chicken wrapped in a smooth, creamy sauce with chili, cheese and walnuts. It's a delicious meal, plus perfect to adapt for leftovers.

Ingredients

  1. 3 ¾ cups cooked chicken 480g, approx (see note 1 to cook from scratch)
  2. 2 slices bread or 3, if small, crusts removed
  3. ⅓ cup milk 80ml
  4. 1 onion
  5. 2 cloves garlic
  6. 3 tablespoon walnuts 28g
  7. 3 tablespoon grated parmesan 8g, or a little more, to taste
  8. 2 tablespoon vegetable oil
  9. 2 teaspoon aji amarillo chili paste
  10. ¼ teaspoon turmeric
  11. ½ cup chicken stock 120ml (or reserved poaching liquid)

Steps to make Aji de gallina

  1. Poach the chicken (if making from scratch) then once cool enough to handle, shred it up (alternatively, shred leftover cooked chicken).
  2. Soak the bread in milk for a few minutes.
  3. Put the soaked bread, walnuts and parmesan in a blender or food processor and blend until smooth.
  4. Soften the onion in a pot then add the garlic, aji amarillo and turmeric and cook a minute more.
  5. Add the bread-walnut mixture, the chicken and stock. Mix well and cook a few more minutes before serving.
